Every engineer eventually ends up here: staring at a blank markdown file, trying to decide how to begin.

So let's just start.

## Why Now

I've been meaning to build a personal site for years. Not just a static resume, but a place to think out loud — to write about the problems I'm solving, the patterns I keep reaching for, and the occasional thing that surprises me.

Writing forces clarity. If you can't explain a decision in prose, you probably don't understand it yet.

## What I'll Write About

Mostly engineering. Specifically:

- **Systems design** — how I think about tradeoffs when architecting services
- **Team dynamics** — what it actually means to lead an engineering team
- **Tools and craft** — things I've learned about the tools I use every day
- **Occasional tangents** — whatever else is on my mind

## How This Site Works

Built with [Astro](https://astro.build), hosted on GitHub Pages. Blog posts are Markdown files committed directly to the repo. To publish: write, push, done.

No CMS. No editorial workflow. Just a text editor and git.
